EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

THE CARE GOVERNANCE DEFICIT: Transforming Social Policy into Auditable Care Metrics in Low- and Middle-Income Countries

Across much of the developing world, social protection systems are entering an unprecedented period of strain. Population ageing is accelerating. Urban migration continues to separate families. Traditional care networks are weakening. Public institutions remain underprepared. The result is an expanding governance vacuum affecting millions of older persons and vulnerable populations. The Care Governance Deficit is a systemic condition in which institutions lack the mechanisms necessary to:

  • ✓ Measure dignity-centred care outcomes
  • ✓ Verify service quality and accountability
  • ✓ Monitor caregiver burden and resilience
  • ✓ Detect abuse, neglect and social isolation
  • ✓ Allocate resources efficiently
  • ✓ Generate real-time policy intelligence

CareBridge'07 Dynamic Global Ltd introduces the Care Governance Architecture (CGA) and the Geriatric Dignity Index (GDI)—a multidimensional framework measuring: Autonomy • Agency • Psychosocial Legacy • Security • Participation. The future of social protection will not be defined by goodwill alone. It will be determined by the ability of institutions to convert compassion into systems, rights into metrics, and care into measurable outcomes.
